### Risk Rationale

- The two largest classes (DslEditorViewModel and MainWindowViewModel) are independently flagged by both the Bloaters and Change Preventers analyses, confirming that their size is not just a cosmetic issue but a structural one that compounds change cost. DslEditorViewModel in particular is the convergence point for 5+ distinct concerns.
- The string-based PropertyChanged subscription pattern across three files creates a fragile coupling web. This is the most architecturally concerning finding because it silently breaks at runtime when WorkspaceState evolves -- no compiler warning, no test failure unless explicitly covered.
- The remaining Minor findings are independent and low-impact individually. The duplicate code patterns and data clumps are typical MVVM friction and do not compound with each other. They become relevant only if the Serious findings are addressed, as refactoring the large classes would naturally resolve several of the Minor items.
